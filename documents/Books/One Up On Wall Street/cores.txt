Course Module 1: The Retail Investor’s Psychological Edge
Building the mental framework required to beat the pros.

Core 1: Flipping the Script on Wall Street

Synthesizes: Chapter 1 (The Amateur), Introduction, and Chapter 18 (The 12 Silliest Things People Say).

The Big Idea: Wall Street is shackled by bureaucracy and short-termism; learn how your flexibility and local knowledge are actually competitive moats, while unlearning the dangerous clichés that trap institutional investors.

Core 2: The Mirror Test & Risk Tolerance

Synthesizes: Chapter 3 (Is This Gambling?), Chapter 4 (Passing the Mirror Test), and Chapter 19 (Options, Futures, and Shorts).

The Big Idea: Before buying a single share, you must determine if you have the stomach for volatility and the financial stability to withstand losses, distinguishing true investing from the casino-like behavior of derivatives.

Core 3: Ignoring the Macro Noise

Synthesizes: Chapter 2 (The Oxymorons), Chapter 5 (Is This a Good Market?), and Chapter 16 (Designing a Portfolio - Market Timing section).

The Big Idea: Stop trying to predict the economy or interest rates; this module teaches you to ignore "The Great Worry" and focus entirely on the micro-economics of specific companies.

Course Module 2: The Art of Discovery
How to find potential "Tenbaggers" in everyday life.

Core 4: Leveraging Your Daily Routine

Synthesizes: Chapter 6 (Stalking the Tenbagger) and Chapter 1 (Specific examples of "Dunkin' Donuts" vs. "High Tech").

The Big Idea: The best stock tips aren't in magazines but in your shopping mall and workplace; learn to spot shifts in consumer behavior before the analysts do.

Core 5: The "Perfect Stock" Profile

Synthesizes: Chapter 8 (The Perfect Stock) and Chapter 9 (Stocks I'd Avoid).

The Big Idea: Hunt for the unsexy, the dull, and the disagreeable—companies that do boring things in niche markets—while avoiding the "hottest stock in the hottest industry."

Core 6: The Six Categories of Opportunity

Synthesizes: Chapter 7 (The Six Categories) and Chapter 17 (Selling strategies specific to categories).

The Big Idea: Not all stocks move the same way; master the classification of companies into Fast Growers, Stalwarts, Slow Growers, Cyclicals, Asset Plays, and Turnarounds to set the right expectations for each.

Course Module 3: Fundamental Analysis & Valuation
The specific numbers and narratives that validate a hunch.

Core 7: The Earnings Engine

Synthesizes: Chapter 10 (Earnings, Earnings, Earnings) and Chapter 11 (The Two-Minute Drill).

The Big Idea: Stock prices ultimately follow earnings; this theme teaches you how to construct the "Two-Minute Drill" monologue that links a company's growth story directly to its earnings potential.

Core 8: Financial Forensics

Synthesizes: Chapter 13 (Some Famous Numbers) and Chapter 12 (Getting the Facts).

The Big Idea: A deep dive into the balance sheet to identify "The numbers that matter," specifically focusing on debt-to-equity ratios, cash positions, and inventory buildup to ensure the company won't go bust.

Core 9: Assessing Management & Dividends

Synthesizes: Chapter 13 (Section on Dividends/Book Value) and Chapter 6 (Visits to HQ).

The Big Idea: How to evaluate if management is shareholder-friendly (through dividends and buybacks) or wasting capital on "diworsification."

Course Module 4: Portfolio Management & Exit Strategy
The mechanics of buying, holding, and selling.

Core 10: Designing the Allocation

Synthesizes: Chapter 16 (Designing a Portfolio) and Chapter 7 (Risk/Reward by Category).

The Big Idea: How to rotate capital between "Stalwarts" for protection and "Fast Growers" for gain, aiming for a 12-15% annual return without over-diversifying into ignorance.

Core 11: The Re-Evaluation Loop

Synthesizes: Chapter 14 (Rechecking the Story) and Chapter 15 (The Final Checklist).

The Big Idea: Investing is dynamic; this module establishes a routine for periodically checking if the original "story" of the stock is still valid or if the fundamentals have deteriorated.

Core 12: The Exit Protocols

Synthesizes: Chapter 17 (The Best Time to Buy and Sell) and Chapter 18 (When prices drop).

The Big Idea: Creating rigid rules for when to fold based on category-specific signals (e.g., inventories rising for a Cyclical) rather than selling out of fear during a market correction.
