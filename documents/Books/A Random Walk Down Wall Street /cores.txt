Course Title: The Efficient Investor Protocol
Core 1: The Valuation Duel

Synthesizes: Part I: Stocks and Their Value (The Firm Foundation Theory vs. The Castles in the Air Theory).

The Big Idea: Master the two warring philosophies of Wall Street—investing based on intrinsic value vs. speculating on crowd psychology—and learn why you need to understand both to survive.

Core 2: Spotting Financial Hallucinations

Synthesizes: Chapter 2 (The Madness of Crowds), Chapter 3 (Stock Valuation from the Sixties to the Nineties), and Chapter 4 (The Biggest Bubble of All: Surfing on the Internet).

The Big Idea: Inoculate yourself against "Get Rich Quick" hysteria by analyzing the anatomy of historical bubbles, from Tulipmania to the Dot-Com crash (and modern Crypto parallels).

Core 3: The Technical Analysis Trap

Synthesizes: Chapter 5 (Technical and Fundamental Analysis) and specific sections debunking chart patterns and momentum trading.

The Big Idea: Why "predicting" the market based on past price charts is financially equivalent to reading tea leaves—and how to stop wasting time on patterns that don't exist.

Core 4: The Fundamental Illusion

Synthesizes: Chapter 6 (Technical Analysis and the Random Walk Theory) and Chapter 7 (How Good Is Fundamental Analysis?).

The Big Idea: Even the "smart" method of analyzing balance sheets has a fatal flaw: random events. Learn why even the best Wall Street analysts rarely beat a coin flip.

Core 5: Respecting the Efficiency Engine

Synthesizes: Part III: The New Technology of Investing (The Efficient Market Hypothesis, Semi-Strong vs. Strong Forms).

The Big Idea: Accept the "Random Walk": Market prices reflect information so quickly that trying to outsmart the collective intelligence of the world is a losing game.

Core 6: The Art of Risk Engineering

Synthesizes: Chapter 8 (A New Walking Shoe: Modern Portfolio Theory) and concepts of Diversification.

The Big Idea: You cannot eliminate risk, but you can manage it. Learn to use "Modern Portfolio Theory" to maximize your returns for every unit of sleep-at-night risk you take.

Core 7: Decoding "Smart" Strategies

Synthesizes: Chapter 11 (Potshots at the Efficient-Market Theory) and Chapter 12 (Smart Beta and Risk Parity).

The Big Idea: An unbiased audit of the "new" ways Wall Street tries to sell you complexity (Factor Investing, Smart Beta) and whether they are worth the fees.

Core 8: Conquering Your Inner Ape

Synthesizes: Chapter 10 (Behavioral Finance) and sections on irrational exuberance.

The Big Idea: Your own brain is your portfolio's worst enemy. Identify the cognitive biases (overconfidence, loss aversion) that cause you to buy high and sell low.

Core 9: The Indexing Manifesto

Synthesizes: Part IV: A Practical Guide and the specific arguments for low-cost Index Funds and ETFs.

The Big Idea: The mathematical proof that a boring, low-fee index fund will outperform 90% of professional hedge fund managers over the long run.

Core 10: The Lifecycle Wealth Map

Synthesizes: Chapter 14 (A Life-Cycle Guide to Investing) and Chapter 15 (Three Giant Steps Down Wall Street).

The Big Idea: A customized asset allocation strategy that evolves with your age—shifting from aggressive growth in your 20s to defensive income in your 60s.
