Course Title: The Fisher Protocol: A Masterclass in Growth Investing


Core 1: The 'Scuttlebutt' Investigation
Synthesizes: "What 'Scuttlebutt' is" (Chapter 2) combined with concepts from "Five Don'ts for Investors" (specifically regarding annual reports and superficial analysis).

The Big Idea: Stop relying on Wall Street analysts and sanitized annual reports; learn the detective work of cross-referencing competitors, suppliers, and former employees to uncover the unvarnished truth about a company.

Core 2: Auditing the Engine (Sales & Innovation)
Synthesizes: Points 1, 2, 3, 8, and 9 from "The Fifteen Points." Focuses strictly on the product and the mechanism of growth.

The Big Idea: A deep dive into determining if a company has a product with "market runway," an R&D department that actually delivers, and a sales organization capable of converting innovation into revenue.

Core 3: Decoding Management DNA
Synthesizes: Points 4, 5, 6, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, and 15 from "The Fifteen Points." Focuses strictly on people and culture.

The Big Idea: How to assess the "integrity factor"—identifying management teams that treat employees well, talk freely when trouble hits (rather than clamming up), and refuse to sacrifice long-term growth for short-term profit margins.

Core 4: Sniper Entries & The Myth of Market Timing
Synthesizes: "When to Buy" (Chapter 3) combined with the "Don't quibble over eighths and quarters" concept.

The Big Idea: Learn to identify the specific scenarios—such as temporary bad news for a solid company or an operational efficiency lag—that create the rare "buy window" for high-quality growth stocks.

Core 5: The Art of the 'Forever Hold'
Synthesizes: "When to Sell" (Chapter 6) combined with "Don't Over-stress Diversification" (Chapter 7/Don't #3).

The Big Idea: Re-training your brain to resist the urge to "take profits" on winners; understanding that true wealth is built by holding a concentrated portfolio of excellent companies through volatility, selling only when the fundamental business model breaks.

Core 6: Immunizing Against Noise
Synthesizes: The remaining "Five Don'ts" and "Five More Don'ts" (e.g., Don't buy because of the annual report tone, Don't follow the crowd, Don't worry about dividends).

The Big Idea: A defensive module on behavioral finance, teaching you to ignore the "optics" that trap amateur investors—such as flashy dividends, stock splits, or the general market sentiment—to focus purely on earnings power.
